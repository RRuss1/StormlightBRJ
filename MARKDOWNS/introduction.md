# Introduction — The Cosmere RPG: Stormlight Handbook

## The Cosmere RPG

The **Cosmere Roleplaying Game** is a tabletop RPG in which you and a group of friends collaboratively tell a story of heroes in a universe of adventure and powerful Investiture. This handbook provides rules for playing in the world of **Roshar** — the setting for the Stormlight Archive novels.

The planet Roshar is part of a larger universe known as the **cosmere**, filled with dozens of inhabited worlds. The Cosmere RPG uses a rules system designed for adventures on any of these worlds.

### Core Books

- **Stormlight Handbook** — Foundational rules: character creation, dice, scene play.
- **Stormlight World Guide** — History, cultures, lore, and adversary profiles.
- **Stonewalkers** — The first official adventure, levels 1–8, written with Brandon Sanderson.

---

## Welcome to Roshar

Roshar is a harsh world wracked by supernatural **highstorms** and eons of conflict. Warlike kingdoms vie for superiority. Flora and fauna have adapted to survive the storms — many sport tough shells or retract into themselves.

But amid this unyielding environment lies great beauty: the highstorms carry **Stormlight**, a well of magic that empowers the world and its inhabitants. Elements and emotions spring to life as **spren** — ethereal beings that fade in and out of the physical world.

### History of Roshar *(Contains Stormlight Archive spoilers)*

- The indigenous **singers** were humanoids with marbled skin and carapace who could attune to Roshar's rhythms.
- Human refugees arrived and eventually made war with the singers, manipulated by the god **Odium**.
- The deity **Honor** opposed Odium; both created immortals — the **Fused** and the **Heralds** — to fight Desolations.
- Humans learned to bond sapient spren, swearing oaths to become the **Knights Radiant**.
- Then the Radiants abandoned their oaths (the **Recreance**), and singers were mentally suppressed into "slaveform."

### The True Desolation *(Contains spoilers)*

- The **listeners** (free singers) summoned the **Everstorm**, freeing singers across Roshar and triggering the True Desolation.
- The evil god **Odium** leads the assault.
- New heroes rise — including new Knights Radiant bonding spren once more.

### Era of Play

The default setting is the **True Desolation era**, just after the events of *Oathbringer*. The *Stonewalkers* adventure begins parallel to *Words of Radiance*.

## What Is a Roleplaying Game?

A roleplaying game is a **collaborative experience** of storytelling and imagination. You and your friends work together to tell the story of a group of characters, rolling dice to determine whether actions succeed or fail.

### Players

Each player (other than the GM) creates a **player character (PC)** — unique to them, with their own origins, ambitions, and decisions.

### Game Master (GM)

One player acts as the **Game Master**, serving as the lead storyteller and referee. The GM:
- Prepares the adventure
- Narrates the action
- Plays all **non-player characters (NPCs)**
- Adjudicates the rules

### Flow of Play

1. **The GM sets the scene** — describes surroundings, context, stakes.
2. **Players decide what to do** — individually or as a group.
3. **The GM narrates results** — sometimes calling for dice rolls to resolve uncertain outcomes.

## Using Cosmere RPG Dice

The game uses polyhedral dice: **d4, d6, d8, d10, d12, d20**, plus a custom **plot die**.

### Rolling a Skill Test

1. Pick a skill and roll the **d20**.
2. Add your **skill modifier**.
3. Add any **bonuses or penalties**.
4. Compare to the **difficulty class (DC)**.
5. Resolve results.

### The Plot Die

The plot die has 6 sides:
- **2 blank sides** — no side effect
- **2 Opportunity (○) sides** — a beneficial side effect
- **2 Complication (C) sides** — a negative side effect, but with a +2 or +4 bonus to the roll

The GM **raises the stakes** on important tests by adding the plot die to the roll.

## Opportunities and Complications

These narrative tools add twists to the story regardless of success or failure.

### Spending an Opportunity
- **Aid an Ally** — next ally test gains advantage
- **Collect Yourself** — recover 1 focus
- **Critically Hit** — change a hit into a critical hit
- **Influence the Narrative** — a positive story effect (GM must approve)

### Facing a Complication
- **Hinder an Ally** — next PC test gains a disadvantage
- **Become Distracted** — lose 1 focus
- **Influence the Narrative** — a negative story effect (GM's choice)

### Complication Bonus
A Complication from the plot die grants a **+2 or +4 bonus** to the current d20 roll.

### Opportunity and Complication Ranges
- Default **Opportunity range**: natural 20
- Default **Complication range**: natural 1
- Some effects can **expand** these ranges

## Actions and Reactions

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| ▶ | 1 action |
| ▶▶ | 2 actions |
| ▶▶▶ | 3 actions |
| ▷ | Free action |
| ↻ | Reaction |
| ★ | Special activation |
| ∞ | Always active |

- **Fast Turn** — act before enemies, gain **2 actions**
- **Slow Turn** — act after fast enemies, gain **3 actions**
- **Reactions** — used in response to triggers; typically 1 per round

---

## Game Conventions

- **GM Has Final Say** — on all rules disputes
- **Specific Beats General** — specific rules override general ones
- **Minimum of Zero** — values can't drop below 0 unless specified
- **Round Down** — always round down when dividing, unless noted

---

## Adventuring and Advancement

Characters grow through:
1. **Gaining levels** — representing practice and growth, unlocking new abilities
2. **Achieving goals** — earning powerful rewards like Shardblades, patrons, or spren bonds

### Five Tiers of Play

| Tier | Levels | Focus |
|------|--------|-------|
| 1 | 1–5 | Personal scale; potential spren bond |
| 2 | 6–10 | Higher stakes; Radiant paths growing |
| 3 | 11–15 | Roshar-wide challenges; fighting Fused |
| 4 | 16–20 | Cosmere-scale threats |
| 5 | 21+ | Mastery; cosmere-level legacy |
