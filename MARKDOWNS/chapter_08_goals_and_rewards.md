# Chapter 8: Goals and Rewards

## Purpose and Obstacle

Every character is defined by two fundamental drives:

### Purpose
Your **core need** — what you can't help but act on. Intrinsic, unchanging (unless a major crisis or epiphany occurs).

**Example Purposes:**
- Save Others — defend those who can't protect themselves
- Seek Truth — insatiable curiosity
- Push Yourself to New Heights — discover and surpass your limits
- Find Connection — define yourself by relationships
- Seek Justice — injustice is intolerable
- Preserve Life — all life has intrinsic value

### Obstacle
What **persistently gets in your way**. Also immutable — it doesn't define you, but it always influences you.

**Example Obstacles:**
- Deep Regret — something weighs heavily on your conscience
- Specific Fear — a particular fear that returns unbidden
- Spite — petty grudges, volatile temper, impulsiveness
- Self-Doubt — trouble believing in yourself
- Mistrust of Others — push people away to avoid disappointment

> **Purpose and obstacle don't have specific mechanical effects** — but they shape your goal choices and inform dramatically interesting decisions. When you act true to your purpose or obstacle in a key moment, the GM may raise the stakes.

## Goals

Goals are concrete objectives your character pursues during adventures. They are also the primary mechanism through which you earn **rewards**.

### Creating Goals
Work with your GM. Answer three questions:
1. **Character's Goal:** How does your character understand this?
2. **Player's Goal:** What do I want to see my character do, learn, or experience?
3. **Advancing Your Goal:** What are a few concrete ways to make progress?

**Example Goals:**
- Become a Noble — work toward wealth and power of the noble houses
- Find a Missing Person — desperate search for someone lost
- Fulfill a Specific Oath — importance on keeping your sworn word
- Repay a Debt of Gratitude — honor a past benevolence
- Get Revenge — seek vengeance for a past misdeed

### Goal Milestones
Each goal has **three milestone checkboxes**. The GM marks one when your character makes meaningful progress (roughly once per session, or twice for especially momentous actions).

Track what happened at each milestone in your Notes section to preserve the story of the goal.

### Advancing Goals
- Check in at end of each session
- Downtime activities can also advance goals
- If a goal goes several sessions without progress, discuss with your GM how to integrate it

### Concluding Goals

When all three milestones are filled, you can conclude the goal — at a dramatically appropriate moment. You earn a **reward** and move on to new goals.

Goals can conclude through:
- **Success** — you achieved what you set out to do
- **Growth** — you grew past the goal or realized something more important
- **Failure** — you definitively failed, but learned from it; plan your next goal accordingly

---

## Radiant Ideal Goals

Swearing Ideals are tracked via special Ideal goals — acquired by choosing the corresponding Ideal talent, not by the GM. When completed (three milestones), you speak the Words and gain the Ideal's reward.

---

## Rewards

Rewards are major boons gained by concluding goals or through significant narrative events.

### Goal Rewards
The reward should fit the goal's conclusion. When the narrative result is obvious (win a duel → Shardblade), that's the reward. When it's ambiguous (personal growth), the GM introduces a meaningful reward that fits the story.

### Spontaneous Rewards
The GM can grant rewards at narratively warranted moments, such as winning a wager in a duel or a highstorm-rescue earning a noble's gratitude.

---

## Reward Categories

### Companions

NPCs devoted to your character. They assist you in ways fitting their relationship — from small tasks to risking their lives for you.

**Companion Activities:** May travel with you, live in a specific place, or remain in the background — providing information, supplies, or direct support as appropriate.

**In Scenes:** GM-controlled, but generally follow your character's requests. Use stat blocks from this book's appendix or the Stormlight World Guide.

**Development Bonuses:** Companions below your tier gain stat bonuses.

#### Example Companion Rewards by Tier

| Tier | Role & Number | Examples | Development Bonuses |
|------|--------------|----------|---------------------|
| 1 | 1 Minion | Axehound, soldier | — |
| 2 | 2 Minions or 1 Standard | Guard, horse | Minion: +2 tests, +10 health |
| 3 | 3 Minions or 1 Standard | Ryshadium, Sleepless | Minion: +4, +20 hp; Standard: +2, +10 hp |
| 4+ | 5 Minions or 2 Standard or 1 Boss | Chasmfiend | Minion: +6, +40 hp; Standard: +4, +20 hp |

**Example Companion Types:**
- **Bodyguard** — provides physical protection; use guard stat block at tier 2
- **Guide** — specialized knowledge of a region or topic; use expert stat block at tier 1
- **Scribe** — handles literacy and writing; expert stat block at tier 1
- **Animal** — riding or working animal; use appendix stat blocks

### Ideals

Ideal rewards are unlocked by completing Ideal goals (see Chapter 5). Completing each Ideal gives you Empowered status and the benefits from that talent (surge access, Shardblade/Shardplate, etc.).

---

### Patrons

An NPC who provides **material support** in exchange for your service.

**Patron Benefits:**
1. **Travel Amenities** (when available/appropriate)
2. **A specific bonus** described in their entry

| PC Tier | Patron Amenities |
|---------|-----------------|
| 1 | Comfortable lodgings and travel |
| 2 | Wealthy lodgings and travel |
| 3 | Aristocratic lodgings and travel |
| 4 | Aristocratic lodgings + personal accommodation |

**Patron Expectations:** Your patron expects results. Repeated failures may reduce the resources they're willing to extend.

#### Example Patrons

**Alethi Highprince**  
Authority extends to you in regions of their political sway:

| Tier | Tests Bonus |
|------|-------------|
| 1 | Persuasion: advantage |
| 2 | Intimidation + Persuasion: advantage |
| 3 | Intimidation + Persuasion + Leadership: 2 advantages |
| 4+ | Deception + Intimidation + Persuasion + Leadership: 2 advantages |

*Note: Using the patron's name can attract their enemies (GM can spend Complication).*

**Knight Radiant**  
After spending enough time with your Radiant patron, you may become their **squire** (see Chapter 5).

**Wealthy Trader**  
Provides an **operations budget** per mission:

| Tier | Budget |
|------|--------|
| 1 | 1,000 mk |
| 2 | 5,000 mk |
| 3 | 10,000 mk |
| 4+ | 50,000+ mk |

### Shards

Shardblades and Shardplate are extraordinary rewards available only through major narrative accomplishments or duels.

| Tier | Shard Reward |
|------|-------------|
| 2 | Shardblade |
| 3 | Shardplate |

**Ways to Win Shards:**
- Win a formal duel (Shardblades and Shardplate are often wagered)
- Kill a Shardbearer in battle (dangerous; tradition gives the winner the Shards)
- Receive as a gift sealing a political alliance

---

### Titles

Social titles that carry political privileges, property, or formal positions.

| Tier | Example Titles | Holdings |
|------|---------------|---------|
| 1 | Lieutenant, senior researcher, 6th-dahn lighteyes | Small estate, lab, office |
| 2 | Captain, branch leader, 5th-dahn lighteyes | Military camp, network of labs, large estate |
| 3 | Highmarshal, spymaster, 4th-dahn lighteyes | Large camp, network of safehouses, palatial estate |
| 4+ | General, listener member of the Five, 3rd-dahn lighteyes | Warcamp/fortress, palace |

*GM discretion: certain titles may also grant relevant utility or cultural expertises.*

**Example Title: Captain**  
Commands 50 soldiers. Loyal cadre obeys orders without question; advantage on tests to give difficult orders.

**Example Title: Mid-Ranking Scion (Azish)**  
Access to governmental and religious archives. Authority over lower-ranking scions for research and clerical tasks; advantage on relevant tests.

## Connections

Many rewards take the form of connections with other beings. Record all meaningful connections — companions, patrons, spren bonds — in the "Connections" section of your character sheet.
