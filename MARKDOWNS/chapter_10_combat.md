# Chapter 10: Combat

## Order of Combat

Combat is divided into **rounds** (~10 seconds each). Each round, every participant takes one turn.

### Fast vs. Slow Turns

| Turn Type | Actions | When You Act |
|-----------|---------|--------------|
| **Fast Turn** | ▶▶ (2 actions) | Before enemy fast turns |
| **Slow Turn** | ▶▶▶ (3 actions) | After all fast turns |

You can choose each round. Coordinate with other players if multiple PCs want the same timing; ties broken by highest Speed (then d20 roll).

### Turn Phases (each round)
1. **Fast PC Turns** — any PCs taking fast turns, in any order
2. **Fast NPC Turns** — GM chooses which NPCs go fast
3. **Slow PC Turns** — remaining PCs
4. **Slow NPC Turns** — remaining NPCs/enemies
5. **Begin the next round**

Allied NPCs act during the NPC phases.

### Gaining Actions
At the start of your turn, gain ▶▶ (fast) or ▶▶▶ (slow). Unused actions are lost at end of turn.

### Gaining Reactions
At the **start of combat** (if not Surprised): gain 1 reaction.  
At the **start of each of your turns**: gain 1 reaction.  
Each reaction lasts until the start of your next turn.

### Surprise
At the start of combat, the GM determines who is **Surprised** (often via Stealth vs. Perception or Deception vs. Insight). Surprised characters gain the Surprised condition (see Chapter 9) and remove it after their first turn.

| Action | Cost | Description |
|--------|------|-------------|
| **Aid** | ↻ + 1 focus | Before an ally's test: grant them advantage. Must be realistically positioned to help. |

## Attacking

### Making an Attack

1. **Choose your target** (eligible based on range and line of effect)
2. **Roll the attack test and damage dice simultaneously**
3. **Resolve damage:**

| Result | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Miss** | No damage. Optionally spend 1 focus per target to graze instead. |
| **Graze** | Deal damage equal to damage dice total only (no skill modifier). |
| **Hit** | Deal damage = damage dice + skill modifier. |
| **Critical Hit** | Spend Opportunity: maximize all damage dice values. |

### Ranged Attacks and Reach
- Attacking while within an enemy's reach: **disadvantage**
- Attacking a target within 5 ft. of your ally: **raise the stakes** (Complication can graze the ally)
- Flying or unstable footing: **disadvantage** on ranged attacks

### Creative Maneuvers
Want to disarm, pin, or perform unusual moves? Use the Strike action — describe your intent, and the GM sets a defense and possible disadvantage based on complexity. Success deals normal damage plus narrative effects.

- **Heavy weapons:** Favor environment damage, driving back foes
- **Light weapons:** Favor precision (targeting specific body parts, cutting items free)

## Targeting and Range

### Range
- **Reach:** Up to 5 ft. (some weapons/effects extend this)
- **Short and Long Range:** Within short range = no penalty; within long range = disadvantage; beyond long range = can't target

### Line of Effect
Required for most abilities: an imaginary straight line must connect you to your target without passing through a solid object.

### Sensing Targets
- Unknown location: **disadvantage** on attacks against them; **can't graze**
- Can only target characters you know exist and have some idea of their location

### Influencing Targets
To apply social/influence effects, your target must:
- Be within reasonable communication distance
- Be able to sense you
- Be able to interpret your communication intent (language not always required)

### Area Effects
- Can affect all characters within a physical area simultaneously
- Don't require sensing characters in the area (unlike targeted effects)
- Typically can't penetrate solid obstacles (GM discretion)

### Target Types

| Type | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Character** | PCs, NPCs, animals — any entity with statistics |
| **Object** | Non-character items that can be picked up |
| **Enemy** | Currently opposing you |
| **Ally** | Friendly or neutral to you; you are NOT your own ally |
| **You** | The one the rule is addressed to |
| **Target** | General term — characters or objects |

## Movement and Positioning

### Character Sizes

| Size | Space Controlled |
|------|-----------------|
| Small | 2.5 ft. |
| Medium | 5 ft. |
| Large | 10 ft. |
| Huge | 15 ft. |
| Gargantuan | 20+ ft. |

### Squeezing
You can move through spaces **one size smaller** than you, but become Slowed and attacks against you gain advantage.

### Movement Types

| Type | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **Standard** | Walking, running — uses movement rate |
| **Climbing** | Slowed; may require Athletics test |
| **Crawling** | Slowed |
| **Swimming** | Slowed; may require Athletics in rough water |
| **Jumping** | Free up to your size (horizontal) or half size (vertical); longer jumps require Athletics |
| **Sneaking** | Slowed; must stay out of line of effect |
| **Flying** | Uses flying rate (granted by effects like Gravitation); passes above ground-level obstacles |

### Moving Around Others
- **Through willing characters:** Allowed
- **Through unwilling characters:** Blocked (unless an effect permits it)
- **Ending in another's space:** Never allowed

### Forced Movement
Not affected by the character's movement rate or anything that modifies it. Does not trigger Reactive Strikes.

### Falling
- From 10+ ft.: take 1d6 impact per 10 ft. fallen
- Take damage: land **Prone**
- Being Prone while flying or climbing causes a fall

### Terrain

| Type | Effect |
|------|--------|
| **Cover** | Within 5 ft.: can Brace to add disadvantage to incoming attacks |
| **Difficult Terrain** | Slowed while moving through it |
| **Dangerous Terrain** | Take damage on entry or at start of turn |

**Dangerous Terrain Examples:**

| Terrain | Damage |
|---------|--------|
| Wooden spikes | 1d4 keen |
| Blazing fire | 1d8 energy |
| Highstorm winds | 1d12 impact |

## Variant: Using a Grid

A 1-inch-square grid where each side = 5 ft. works well with standard miniatures.

### Grid Movement
Move from square to square. Horizontal, vertical, and diagonal movement all cost 5 ft. each.

**Corners:** Can't move diagonally past a corner blocked by an obstacle.

### Range on a Grid
Count the shortest path as if moving. For ranged attacks, ignore obstacles a projectile could fly over.

### Line of Effect on a Grid
Draw a line from any corner of your square to any corner of the target's square. If it doesn't cross a solid object, you have line of effect.

### Areas on a Grid
Areas align to grid lines. The origin point can be any space adjacent to your square. Small (2.5-ft.) areas typically still affect one full 5-ft. square.

## Mounted Combat

### Riding
- Mount or dismount using ▶
- While riding, use the **mount's movement rate** (not yours)
- Normally no test to steer; difficult maneuvers may require Survival
- If you roll a Complication while riding, you may fall Prone

### Using Mount Abilities
If you have a utility expertise in riding that mount type, you can activate their stat block abilities using your own resources (focus, ▶, ▷). Their actions happen on your turn — no separate turn for the mount.

Without riding expertise, the mount doesn't act.

### Attacking Mounts
Can target either the rider or the mount. If you trigger a Reactive Strike while mounted, enemies may target your mount.

Making a **melee attack against a rider on a larger mount**: disadvantage unless you're also mounted on a similarly-sized mount.
