# Chapter 3: Character Statistics

## About Your Statistics

Your character sheet is organized into three columns — **Physical, Cognitive, and Spiritual** — reflecting the three facets of cosmere reality.

| Column | Attributes | Skills | Defense | Resources |
|--------|-----------|--------|---------|-----------|
| **Physical** | Strength, Speed | Athletics, Agility, Heavy Weaponry, Light Weaponry, Stealth, Thievery | Physical | Health, Movement Rate, Lifting Capacity |
| **Cognitive** | Intellect, Willpower | Crafting, Deduction, Discipline, Intimidation, Lore, Medicine | Cognitive | Focus, Recovery Die |
| **Spiritual** | Awareness, Presence | Deception, Insight, Leadership, Perception, Persuasion, Survival | Spiritual | Investiture, Senses Range |

**Lifting and Carrying Capacity:**

| Strength | Lifting Capacity | Carrying Capacity |

| 0 | 100 lb. | 50 lb. |

## Defenses

Three defense values protect you from unwanted effects. Each sets the **DC** for tests made against you.

| Defense | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| **Physical** | 10 + Strength + Speed + bonuses/penalties |
| **Cognitive** | 10 + Intellect + Willpower + bonuses/penalties |
| **Spiritual** | 10 + Awareness + Presence + bonuses/penalties |

When a character makes a skill test against you, they typically use the defense matching their skill's category (physical, cognitive, or spiritual).

---

## Deflect

Your **deflect value** reduces **energy, impact, and keen** damage by that amount.

- If your deflect is 2 and you take 5 energy damage → you take **3 damage**.
- Deflect also provides some protection from serious injuries.
- Deflect does **not** reduce spirit or vital damage.

## Expertises

Expertises represent niche knowledge gained from upbringing, training, and experience.

### Using Expertises

**Assumed Knowledge:** You automatically know basic facts about your area of expertise — no test needed.

**Advanced Thinking:** Your depth allows you to make tests that would be impossible for others. Success may grant more information than an inexperienced character would learn.

**Expert Crafting:** Some specialized items require a specific expertise to craft.

**Item Expert Traits:** Some weapons/armor grant additional benefits if you have the relevant expertise.

**Known Languages:** Cultural expertises grant fluency in that culture's language(s).

---

### Expertise Categories

| Category | Description | How to Gain |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Armor** | Knowledge of maintaining and wearing a specific armor type | Choose freely; grants expert traits |
| **Cultural** | Regional, social, and linguistic knowledge | Choose from Chapter 2 cultures |
| **Utility** | Tools, trades, technical fields (e.g., Engineering, Military Strategy) | Choose freely |
| **Weapon** | Maintaining and wielding a specific weapon type | Choose freely; grants expert traits |
| **Specialist** | Restricted fields (Shardblades, Shardplate, Fabrial Crafting, etc.) | Only via talents, rewards, or GM permission; can't choose from Intellect |

## Health, Focus, and Investiture

### Health
Physical stamina and resistance to minor wounds.

> **Maximum Health = 10 + Strength + bonuses/penalties**

- At 0 health: become **Unconscious** and suffer an injury.
- Taking damage at 0 health causes additional injuries.
- Recover partially during short rest; fully during long rest.

### Focus
Mental resolve and ability to resist distraction.

> **Maximum Focus = 2 + Willpower + bonuses/penalties**

- Spent to fuel talents, abilities, and resist social influence.
- Recover partially during short rest; fully during long rest.

### Investiture
Spiritual resource for Radiant abilities (Stormlight on Roshar).

> **Maximum Investiture = 2 + higher of Awareness or Presence + bonuses/penalties**

- Unlocked when you choose a Radiant path's First Ideal talent.
- Spent to activate surges and spren bond talents.

## Senses

### Senses Range
When your primary sense is obscured (pitch darkness, howling storm), you can still perceive within your **senses range** using other faculties. Outside that range, targets cannot be detected.

### Sensing Hidden Characters and Objects
Good senses don't automatically pierce deception. Stealth and illusion require standard skill tests to detect.

### Senses and Disabilities
If you're accustomed to limited senses (e.g., a blind character), you rely on other senses and make Perception tests the same as others. You can work with your GM to optionally add mechanical implications.

## Skills

### Using Skills

When you attempt something with a chance of failure, the GM calls for a **skill test**:
- Roll d20
- Add skill modifier
- Compare to DC

Succeed ≥ DC | Fail < DC

You don't always need ranks — but more ranks mean a higher modifier and better odds.

### Skill Ranks and Modifiers

> **Skill Modifier = attribute score + ranks in skill**

You gain skill ranks at character creation and each time you gain a level.

### Difficulty Class

| Difficulty | DC |
|------------|----|
| Easy | 10 |
| Medium | 15 |
| Hard | 20 |
| Very Hard | 25 |
| Nearly Impossible | 30 |

**Against Defenses:** When testing against another character, the DC is usually their corresponding defense value. The GM may modify this up or down based on circumstances.

**Opposed Tests:** Both characters roll their relevant skill; the higher result wins (ties favor the defender).

**Automatic Successes:** Some effects skip the roll entirely — no Opportunities or Complications.

### Advantages and Disadvantages

**Advantage:** Roll two of the chosen die; pick the better result.  
**Disadvantage:** The GM chooses a die; roll two; the GM picks the worse result.  
Multiple advantages/disadvantages cancel each other out (1:1).

### Varied Test Results

- **Degree of Success/Failure:** The gap between result and DC informs narrative flavor.
- **Opportunities and Complications:** From plot die or natural 20/1.
- **Trying Again:** Possible but may cost focus, health, or carry escalating risks.

### Resisting Influence
When you succeed on a socially oriented test, the target can spend **2 focus** (or **4 focus** for a very strong argument) to resist your influence.

### Working Together

- **In Combat:** Use the Aid reaction (spend 1 focus, grant advantage).
- **Outside Combat:** The leader makes one test, gaining advantage for each helper. Helpers must be able to reasonably contribute.

## The Eighteen Skills

### Physical Skills *(Strength or Speed)*

**Agility (Speed):** Maneuver in your environment, perform acrobatics, pilot vehicles, steer mounts.  
- *Gain Advantage with Agility by:* Rolling at an unexpected angle, throwing an opponent off balance, slipping inside their guard.

**Athletics (Strength):** Physical prowess — lifting, climbing, jumping, swimming, unarmed attacks.  
- Jumping without a test: horizontal = your size; vertical = half your size. Longer jumps require Athletics (DC set by GM; 40-ft jump is DC 30 for a Medium character).
- *Gain Advantage with Athletics by:* Leaping onto a foe, seizing their shield, flexing menacingly.

**Heavy Weaponry (Strength):** Wield axes, hammers, most Shardblades, and other heavy weapons.  
- *Gain Advantage by:* Winding up a dangerous swing, dramatically drawing a weapon, performing a kata.

**Light Weaponry (Speed):** Wield knives, short swords, bows, slings, and other light weapons.  
- *Gain Advantage by:* Feinting with a flourish, patting a concealed weapon, throwing a weapon as distraction.

**Stealth (Speed):** Sneak, hide, blend into a crowd, avoid notice.  
- In combat: Use a Skill (▶) to hide. Must be in cover, obscured area, or outside line of effect. Test against target's Spiritual defense.
- To search: Perception test, DC = your Stealth result (disadvantage if you're in cover/obscured).
- *Gain Advantage by:* Ducking out of view, using hidden items, feinting.

**Thievery (Speed):** Pickpocket, lockpick, sleight of hand, escape bonds, tie knots, create disguises.  
- Pickpocketing in combat: Raise the stakes; DC = target's Spiritual defense; disadvantage if target is aware.
- *Gain Advantage by:* Making an item "disappear," drawing a concealed weapon, donning a shocking disguise.

---

### Cognitive Skills *(Intellect or Willpower)*

**Crafting (Intellect):** Design and build physical objects — simple tools, machines, pottery, artwork.  
- Complex items (weapons, armor, fabrials) require an expertise.
- *Gain Advantage by:* Making a powderbomb, attaching rope to a weapon, deploying an improvised light-flash device.

**Deduction (Intellect):** Understand the world through logic, observation, and evidence.  
- *Gain Advantage by:* Inferring a foe's weakness, analyzing rhetoric for true motives, identifying a foe's key asset.

**Discipline (Willpower):** Control outward reactions — maintain composure through fear, anger, pain, sorrow.  
- Can be reactive (shake off fear) or active (put on a brave face).
- *Gain Advantage by:* Waiting for the perfect moment, holding firm, marching into danger unflinching.

**Intimidation (Willpower):** Induce fear and ensure compliance through dominance or threats.  
- Insincere but plausible threats work normally. Preposterous bluffs impose disadvantage.
- *Gain Advantage by:* Humming to contentious rhythms (singers), mocking an enemy, displaying signs of the Thrill.

**Lore (Intellect):** Recall history, folklore, religion, current events, science, and places.  
- Expertises allow automatic recall of basic facts; testing Lore without one may give incomplete information.
- *Gain Advantage by:* Demonstrating inconsistencies in a target's beliefs, recalling weaknesses, recognizing useful environmental properties.

**Medicine (Intellect):** Heal, diagnose, support mental/physical illness, determine cause of death.  
- **In Combat (1+ ranks):** Use a Skill (▶▶), spend 2 focus, DC 15 test; success = target recovers Medicine ranks in health.
- **During Long Rest:** DC 20; reduce a shallow/vicious injury duration by 1d4 days (only once per rest per character).
- *Gain Advantage by:* Spotting a previous injury to exploit, identifying a useful medicine, alleviating pain for an ally.

---

### Spiritual Skills *(Awareness or Presence)*

**Deception (Presence):** Mislead through dishonesty, manipulation, deflection, or body language.  
- Long-distance deception (via writing, spanreed): Disadvantage.
- *Gain Advantage by:* Pretending to aim elsewhere, wearing an enemy uniform, rattling a foe's composure.

**Insight (Awareness):** Discern true feelings, see through deceptions, read social situations.  
- *Gain Advantage by:* Provoking a revealing response, following a hunch about posturing, appealing to a conflicting desire.

**Leadership (Presence):** Inspire people, command allies, delegate tasks, bait enemies.  
- Different from Persuasion: Leadership appeals to higher ideals and commands situations; Persuasion uses logic and self-interest.
- *Gain Advantage by:* Challenging an enemy to a duel, calling allies to cover your advance, asking a companion for help.

**Perception (Awareness):** Notice details about surroundings, search for hidden characters/items.  
- Works regardless of which primary sense you use.
- *Gain Advantage by:* Spotting an environmental advantage, gauging exact distance, observing weakness in an enemy's stance.

**Persuasion (Presence):** Charm, debate, reason, barter, appeal to self-interest.  
- Targets are more receptive if they already like and trust you (GM may lower DC).
- *Gain Advantage by:* Negotiating duel terms, distracting with banter, making a genuine offer of peace.

**Survival (Awareness):** Obtain resources, avoid environmental threats, understand animal behavior, track.  
- **Foraging (Short Rest):** Test Survival; on success, identify and collect useful natural resources.
- *Gain Advantage by:* Coordinating with an animal companion, using terrain knowledge for a vantage point, adjusting for weather.
