# Chapter 9: Adventuring

## Roleplaying

Adventures unfold via a repeating cycle:
1. **GM sets the scene** — describes surroundings, context, stakes
2. **Players decide what to do**
3. **GM narrates results** — sometimes calling for dice rolls

### First-Person vs. Third-Person Roleplaying

**First-Person:** You speak *as* your character — embodying their voice, expressions, and reactions. Immersive; reveals emotions directly.

**Third-Person:** You describe your character by name, narrating their actions and thoughts. Great for emotionally charged scenes or separating your values from your character's behavior.

---

## Time

Time flows at whatever pace the story needs. The GM zooms in and out:

- **"Zoomed out"** — montages, travel, downtime; large amounts of time pass
- **"Zoomed in"** (scenes) — every heartbeat matters; individual actions are tracked

### What Your Character Knows
Distinguish between **player knowledge** and **character knowledge**. When you know more than your character, use it to set up dramatic irony — not to shortcut consequences. When your character knows more than you, ask your GM for context.

---

## Scenes

When actions "zoom in," you're in a **scene**. Three types:

| Scene Type | Focus | Chapter |
|-----------|-------|---------|
| **Combat** | Physically hostile conflict | Chapter 10 |
| **Conversation** | Social interaction with uncertain, meaningful outcomes | Chapter 11 |
| **Endeavor** | Traversal, exploration, discovery, pursuit | Chapter 12 |

Statistics carry over between scenes until you have a chance to rest.

### Transitioning Between Scenes
When one scene's goal is accomplished (or failed), move to the next. Each resolution should open new questions and drive the story forward.

---

## Events

Events are optional storytelling tools that count down to a dramatic moment by tracking **Opportunities and Complications**.

### Event Tracks
The GM places an event card on the table with **Opportunity (○) and/or Complication (C) slots**. Players and the GM can fill these by spending their plot die results.

- **Opportunity:** Player spends their rolled ○ to fill one slot toward a positive event
- **Complication:** GM spends their rolled C to fill one slot toward a negative event
- **NPCs:** Ally Complications fill positive event slots; enemy Opportunities fill negative ones

When all slots of one type are filled, the event **triggers**.

---

## Downtime

Between scenes, characters engage in **downtime** activities.

### Introducing Downtime
The GM tells you how much time you have and any narrative restrictions (e.g., must stay in a locked city).

### Resolving Downtime
Each player describes their intended activity. The GM works through each activity's outcome, calling for skill tests where relevant. Results may be narrative, monetary, items, or goal progress.

### Downtime Activity Ideas

**Crafting**  
Use downtime for complex item/fabrial creation (see Chapter 7).

**Working a Profession**  
Earn income or meet patron obligations. Simple activities may not require a test.

**Recuperating**  
Each day of dedicated rest-and-recovery counts as **2 days** toward healing an injury.

**Researching**  
Investigate mysteries, study topics, seek forgotten knowledge. Usually 10+ days; costs 3 mk/day. Requires relevant skill tests (Deduction, Lore, Persuasion).

**Training**  
Learn a new expertise (including specialist ones like Warhammer or Half-Shard). Usually 20+ days; costs 5 mk/day. Requires a relevant skill test; failure adds time/complications.

**Self-Reflection**  
After 10+ days and 5 mk/day, you can **rebuild your character** using the Starting at Higher Levels rules — reassigning talents, skill ranks, etc. (with GM approval and maintaining all prerequisites).

## Conditions

Conditions alter your abilities until they end. They apply only to characters, not objects (unless the GM decides otherwise).

| Condition | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Afflicted** | Take specified damage at end of each turn (combat) or every 10 seconds (out of combat). Stacks — multiple Afflicted effects resolve separately. |
| **Determined** | Once per trigger: when you fail a test, add an Opportunity to the result. Condition then ends. |
| **Disoriented** | Can't use reactions. Senses always count as obscured. Perception tests gain disadvantage. |
| **Empowered** | Advantage on all tests. Investiture refills to max at start of each turn. Ends at end of current scene. |
| **Enhanced** | Attribute bonus as specified (e.g., Enhanced [Strength +1]). Does NOT change defenses, health, focus, or Investiture max. Can stack different attributes; multiple Enhanced conditions of same attribute are additive. |
| **Exhausted [−X]** | Apply −X penalty to all test results. Reduce penalty by 1 after each long rest; removed when penalty = 0. Cumulative — additional Exhausted conditions add to current penalty. |
| **Focused** | Abilities that cost focus have their cost reduced by 1. |
| **Immobilized** | Movement rate = 0; can't move or be moved. |
| **Prone** | Lying flat. You are Slowed. Melee attacks against you gain advantage. Can Brace without cover. Stand up as ▷ (movement −5 ft. until start of next turn). Falling Prone while flying or climbing causes a fall. |
| **Restrained** | Movement rate = 0. Disadvantage on all tests except to escape. |
| **Slowed** | Movement rate halved. |
| **Stunned** | In combat: lose reactions; on your turn, gain 2 fewer ▶ and no reaction. Out of combat: move and react slower at GM's discretion. |
| **Surprised** | Lose reactions; no reaction at combat start or turn start; can't take fast turn; gain 1 fewer ▶. Removed after your next turn. |
| **Unconscious** | Movement = 0; can't communicate or take any actions/reactions except Breathe Stormlight and Regenerate (if Radiant). Fall Prone; drop held items; unaware of surroundings. PCs can choose to regain consciousness at end of any of their turns, or when healed to 1+ health. NPCs auto-revive when healed to 1+ health. |

---

## Durations

Effects that last a number of rounds: note which phase (fast PC, fast NPC, slow PC, slow NPC) the effect began in. It lasts until the beginning of that same phase the next round.

"End of your next turn" — ends when that turn ends, regardless of turn speed.

---

## Food and Water

**Food:** Can go without for Willpower score days before suffering ill effects. Each additional day without food: Exhausted [−1]. Die at Exhausted [−10] or lower. Eating a day's worth resets the count.

**Water:** Each day without sufficient water: Exhausted [−1]. Die at Exhausted [−10] or lower.

## Damage, Injury, and Death

### Damage Types

| Type | Reduced by Deflect? | Common Sources |
|------|--------------------|-|
| Energy | Yes | Fire, lightning |
| Impact | Yes | Hammers, boulders |
| Keen | Yes | Swords, spikes |
| Spirit | **No** | Shardblades, some surges |
| Vital | **No** | Poison, suffocation, extreme cold |

### Taking Damage
- Reduce current health by damage amount (deflect reduces energy/impact/keen first)
- At 0 health: become **Unconscious** and suffer an injury
- Taking damage at 0 health: suffer another injury

### Injuries

When you suffer an injury, make an **injury roll** (d20):

**Modifiers:**
- Add your deflect value
- Add relevant talents/abilities
- Subtract 5 for each **existing injury**

| Roll | Duration |
|------|----------|
| 16+ | **Flesh Wound** — lasts until after a long rest |
| 6–15 | **Shallow Injury** — lasts 1d6 days |
| 1–5 | **Vicious Injury** — lasts 6d6 days |
| −5 to 0 | **Permanent Injury** — permanent without supernatural intervention |
| −6 or lower | **Death** |

### Injury Effects (roll d8 or choose)

| d8 | Effect | Narrative Examples |
|----|--------|-|
| 1–2 | Exhausted [−1] | General stamina loss |
| 3 | Exhausted [−2] | Severe stamina loss |
| 4–5 | Slowed | Injured leg, reduced speed |
| 6 | Disoriented | Head injury, overwhelming trauma |
| 7 | Surprised | Shock from the injury |
| 8 | Can only use one hand | Injured arm |

### Recovering From Injuries

- **Temporary injuries** heal after the specified duration (or 2× speed during recuperating downtime)
- **Permanent injuries** require supernatural healing (like Progression's Injury Regrowth)
- Characters can adapt to injuries, eventually removing mechanical effects (representing adjustment)

### Injuries vs. Disabilities
The injury system isn't for modeling pre-existing disabilities. Characters with disabilities navigate the world using their full skill suite unless they choose otherwise — work with your GM on any desired mechanical representation.

### Minor NPCs and Injuries
When a minor NPC suffers an injury, they're immediately **defeated**. The PC who dealt the blow decides if they die or fall Unconscious. NPCs with significant narrative roles may not be defeated automatically.

## Death

When a PC dies, they cling to consciousness long enough for **final words**. Then their soul passes to Shadesmar briefly before moving on.

**Returning to Life:** Possible via the Old Magic or Progression's *From the Brink* talent. The soul must be willing.

> **GM Tip:** PC deaths are most satisfying when they result from player choice (heroic sacrifice), not random chance. Warn players out-of-character when a choice could lead to certain death.

## Resting

### Short Rest
Any uninterrupted 1+ hour period of relaxation.

**Benefits (choose one):**
- Roll your **recovery die** and add to current health, focus, or a combination
- **Tend to Others** — allies can add your Medicine modifier to their recovery die
- **Forage** — use Survival to find resources
- Other reasonable non-test activities

Some talents also activate during a short rest (and can stack with the above).

### Long Rest
Any uninterrupted 8+ hour period of meaningful rest (including ~6+ hours of sleep).

**Benefits:**
- Recover **all lost health and focus**
- Exhausted penalty **reduces by 1**

Some talents activate on a long rest as well.
