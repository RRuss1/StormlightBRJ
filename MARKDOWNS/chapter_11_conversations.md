# Chapter 11: Conversations

## Setting the Scene

Any scene can transition into a conversation. The GM briefly describes:
- Who you're talking to
- What you want to achieve
- What the other party's general motives and desires are (as you'd know them)

---

## Order of Conversations

Conversations are less structured than combat — no formal initiative. Players and GM contribute one at a time. When everyone has had a chance to contribute, that's one **round**.

---

## Contributions

A **contribution** is any meaningful action in a conversation. This includes:
- Attempting to influence someone
- Helping or hindering another's efforts
- Bolstering an ally's resolve
- Gathering information
- Interjecting flavor

### Contribution Outcomes

| Outcome | Typical Test |
|---------|-------------|
| **Influence a Person** | Skill test vs. Spiritual or Cognitive defense (DC may be adjusted by relationship) |
| **Help or Hinder** | Grant ally advantage or impose enemy disadvantage on their next test |
| **Bolster an Ally** | Target recovers focus |
| **Gather Information** | Test vs. Spiritual or Cognitive, or info given freely |
| **Interject Flavor** | No test needed |

**When to test:** If outcome is uncertain or skill-dependent, the GM calls for a test (usually against the target's corresponding defense).

---

## Focus in Conversations

**Focus** represents resolve and patience in social settings.

### Who Can You Influence?
Any character you can communicate with effectively (within sensing range, can interpret your intent).

### Resisting Influence
When you succeed on a social test, the target can spend focus to ignore your influence:
- **Reasonably persuasive:** 2 focus to resist
- **Extremely persuasive:** 4 focus to resist

Some talent effects (not just tests) can also be resisted by spending 2 focus.

### Running Out of Focus
An NPC at 0 focus becomes **too frustrated or scattered to resist further influence**. They may concede or check out of the conversation.

As a PC, running low on focus is a cue to change tactics — fight, flee, or try something different.

---

## Conversation Types

### Inquiry
*One party tries to acquire information; the other tries to withhold it.*

**Opportunities & Complications:**

| Opportunity | Complication |
|-------------|--------------|
| Discover shared objective or enemy | Accidentally reveal more than intended (or spend 2 focus to resist) |
| Set the subject at ease (advantage on next test) | Offend the subject (disadvantage on next test) |
| Distraction creates momentary disarray | Someone interrupts with an unrelated request |

---

### Negotiation
*Two or more parties try to obtain something from each other.*

**Opportunities & Complications:**

| Opportunity | Complication |
|-------------|--------------|
| Notice subtle clues about their actual resources | Tip your hand about your resources (or spend 2 focus to resist) |
| Recall a law or custom in your favor | Other party reveals a barrier out of their control |
| Learn they could get what you need elsewhere | Another bidder enters, threatening to raise the price |

---

### Scheme
*At least one party is trying to dupe another (the "mark") who believes they're in a different kind of conversation.*

If the scheme is exposed, the conversation likely ends — possibly in combat.

**Opportunities & Complications:**

| Opportunity | Complication |
|-------------|--------------|
| Mark reveals something useful for your cover story | One of your lies unravels unexpectedly |
| A neutral NPC corroborates your story | A neutral NPC is revealed as an agent of the opposition |
| An ally appears in a plausible disguise | Someone from your past shows up at the worst moment |

---

### Social
*Characters seek to bond, impress, or build connections. May harbor hidden agendas.*

**Opportunities & Complications:**

| Opportunity | Complication |
|-------------|--------------|
| Notice a hint of someone's hidden emotional state | Commit a subtle cultural blunder |
| Relax and recover 1d4 focus | Become drained or aggravated; lose 1 focus |
| Impress someone (inclined to help you later) | Spot a goal opportunity that would disrupt the conversation |
