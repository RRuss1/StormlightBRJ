# Chapter 2: Origins

## Considering Your Origins

Your character's story begins long before your adventures start. Think about:
- Who and where they come from
- What they've spent time learning (voluntarily or under duress)
- How personality informs interests
- What story arc you want to set up

Consider: occupation, identity, friends and factions, relationships.

> **Culture as a Canvas, Not the Painting:** No group of people is a monolith. Your culture is a set of stimuli you've been exposed to — how your character responds to those values and traditions is entirely up to you.

---

## Ancestry

Your ancestry reflects your species and shapes appearance, size, and special traits.

### Human Ancestry

Most of Roshar is human-dominated. Humans are Medium size.

**Ancestry Bonus Talents (Levels 1, 6, 11, 16, 21):**
At creation and at the start of each new tier, humans gain one bonus talent chosen from any **heroic path**. Standard prerequisites apply.

### Singer Ancestry

Singers are the indigenous people of Roshar. They have marbled skin (black, white, or red), varied hairstrands (orange, red, black), and protective carapace. Singers are Medium size.

**Unique Talent Tree (Level 1):** Access to the Singer talent tree.

**Change Form (Level 1):** Gain *Change Form (Singer Key)* plus one bonus form talent. Start in a chosen form.

**Ancestry Bonus Talents (Levels 6, 11, 16, 21):** At each new tier, gain one talent from the Singer tree or any heroic path.

## Singer Talent Tree

### Change Form (Singer Key)
*Prerequisite: Singer ancestry | ▶▶*

During a highstorm, you can bond a spren and change into any form you've unlocked. While in a form, you gain its bonuses (attribute increases, actions, etc.). You can only be in one form at a time.

Starting forms: **Dullform** (no bonded spren; can pass as a "parshman") and **Mateform** (bonded lifespren; specialized for reproduction).

## Culture

Cultural expertises are chosen in Step 1 of character creation (up to two). They can be tied to ancestry or shaped by where you've lived, studied, or traveled.

### Rosharan Cultures

#### Alethi
- Ruled by a monarch and ten highprinces
- Strictly enforced gender roles; lighteyes vs. darkeyes caste system
- Most men are illiterate; women keep their left hand covered (safehand)
- **Expertise:** Know princedoms, highprinces, regional dialects; familiar with Vorinism, the Almighty, and the Heralds

#### Azish
- Bureaucratic nation; led by the Prime Aqasix and viziers
- Independent thought encouraged in debate, but procedure is paramount
- **Expertise:** Know the bureaucracy, Bronze Palace, member states; navigate civic buildings; familiar with Kadasixes and scions

#### Herdazian
- Verdant nation known for hog ranching; large diaspora
- Ancient singer ancestry → crystalline fingernails (can function like flint), resistance to native Rosharan poisons
- Vorinism practiced personally, less formally
- **Expertise:** Know king and ranching families; navigate coastal areas; know sparkflickers; familiar with Herdazian Vorinism

#### Iriali
- Ruled by a Triumvirate; follow the religion of the One and the Long Trail
- Golden skin and hair; value diverse experiences on a destined journey
- **Expertise:** Know the Triumvirate; one home region; familiar with the One and the Long Trail; know travel management

#### Kharbranthian
- City-state; center of academic and medical achievement
- Free healthcare; home to the Palanaeum (largest library on Roshar)
- Human slavery banned (but not for singers)
- **Expertise:** Know education systems; know common medicines and hygiene; familiar with Vorinism

#### Listener
- Free singers of the Shattered Plains; retained identity through oral tradition
- Opposed the return of Odium's influence; assassinated King Gavilar
- Generally only available to singer characters
- **Expertise:** Know dullform through workform; familiar with the Five; know some listener songs; know Shattered Plains ecology

#### Natan
- Remnant of a destroyed empire; some live as nomads
- Pale blue skin and white hair
- **Expertise:** Heard tales of the lost Natan empire; know highstorm defenses; familiar with Vorinism and Natan moon reverence

#### Reshi
- Island nation aboard Tai-na greatshell carapaces
- Worship greatshell islands as gods; tourism common
- **Expertise:** Know Tai-na, spren deities, island war tales; familiar with Reshi Sea ecology; know resource sourcing

#### Shin
- Isolated western nation; Stone Shamans govern; stone is sacred
- Farmers are esteemed; warriors are lowly
- **Truthless** must carry an Oathstone and serve whoever holds it
- **Expertise:** Know mild highstorms, Shinovar ecology, bartering customs; familiar with Stone Shamanism

#### Thaylen
- Island nation; merchant councils elect the monarch; trade-focused
- Long white eyebrows; more relaxed Vorin practice
- **Expertise:** Know elective monarchy, merchant councils, artifabrian guilds; know sea travel; familiar with Vorinism and the Passions

#### Unkalaki (Horneaters)
- Mountain-dwellers; ruled by nuatoma patriarchs; crater lake communities
- Strong teeth from singer ancestry; some can see spren others cannot (Sighted)
- **Expertise:** Know mountain ecology and myths; acclimate to thin air; familiar with water/mountain/tree gods; know of offworld visitors

#### Veden
- Ruled by king and highprinces; Vorin faith centered at the Holy Enclave
- Four ethnic groups (Bav, Siln, Veden, Unkalaki); sons named by birth order
- **Expertise:** Know princedoms and ethnic groups; familiar with Vorinism and current church events

#### Wayfarer
- Not nation-specific; life of travel, trade routes, and long distances
- **Expertise:** Read international maps; find currency exchange; know basic greetings and cultural touchstones from various nations

#### Other Cultures
With GM approval, you can choose other cultural expertises:
- Other Rosharan nations (create your own expertise)
- **Subcultures:** High Society, Military Life, Underworld, etc.

## Singers in Human Cultures

### Previously Enslaved Singers
Before the Everstorm, most singers across Roshar were trapped in **slaveform** — their minds suppressed. After the Everstorm freed them, enslaved singers awakened to the full horror of what had been done to them.

Consider:
- How was your character harmed by their enslavement?
- How do they feel toward humans now?
- How has recovering access to suppressed rhythms affected them?

### Listeners
The listener enclave near the Shattered Plains retained their identity. Consider:
- What exposure to humans has your character had?
- How do they feel about humans now, given the War of Reckoning?
