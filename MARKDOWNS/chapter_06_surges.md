# Chapter 6: Surges

## Using Surges

Surges are the ten magical forces wielded by the Knights Radiant through Stormlight. Each Radiant order controls two surges based on their bonded spren.

### Surge Skills
Each surge is a **skill** associated with a specific attribute. Add the corresponding attribute to your surge ranks to get your surge modifier.

You cannot use a surge until you have **at least 1 rank** in it (granted when you speak the First Ideal). Gain additional ranks the same way you do for other skills.

**On your character sheet:** When you gain your first rank in a surge skill, write it on the blank lines beneath your skills.

### Surge Scaling

| Ranks | Die Size | Effect Size |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | d4 | Small (2.5 ft.) |
| 2 | d6 | Medium (5 ft.) |
| 3 | d8 | Large (10 ft.) |
| 4 | d10 | Huge (15 ft.) |
| 5 | d12 | Gargantuan (20 ft.) |

### Surge Attacks
Follow standard attack rules:
- Disadvantage if you can't sense the target
- On a hit: add surge modifier to damage; spend Opportunity to critical hit
- On a miss: spend 1 focus per target to graze instead

### Surge Infusions and Durations

**Infusing an Object:** Spend Investiture upfront; infusion expends 1 Investiture each round (at start of your turn).

**Infusing Yourself:** Spend needed Investiture each round automatically; end anytime as a free action.

**Duration Outside Combat:**
- Combat: 1 Investiture per round (~10 seconds)
- Conversation: 1 Investiture per round of contributions (~1 minute)
- Endeavor: 1 Investiture per round (~1–10 minutes)
- Outside scenes: 1 Investiture per ~10 minutes

**Adding to an Infusion:** At the start of your turn, spend ▷ to infuse additional Investiture to extend an active effect.

**Ending a Surge:** When the infusion runs out, or you end it voluntarily as a free action.

## Abrasion *(Speed)*

**Orders:** Dustbringer, Edgedancer  
*The surge that alters friction, usually by nearly eliminating it.*

### Abrasion Surge *(▶)*
Spend 1+ Investiture to infuse an object/surface within reach (must have a hand free and touch the target), making it **nearly frictionless**. Expends 1 Investiture per round.

Can't exceed your surge size. Can't target characters, Invested objects, or Stormlight-infused objects.

**Infusing yourself (any size):** Spend 1 Investiture per round. While infused:
- **Skate (▷):** Spend 2 focus; Move in a straight line on the ground.
- **Slide:** Count as one size smaller when squeezing; advantage on escaping friction/pressure restraints.

**Abrasion on Others:** To infuse an unwilling target's held/worn item or the ground supporting them, succeed on an Abrasion test vs. their Physical defense.

### Abrasion Talents

**Frictionless Motion ∞** *(Speak the First Ideal)*  
While infused with Abrasion: movement +10 ft.; ignore Slowed from difficult terrain, climbing, crawling, swimming.

**Reverse Abrasion ★** *(Speak the First Ideal)*  
Choose to **increase** friction instead. Targets gain advantage on Agility/Athletics in that area; Slowed condition from difficult terrain is ignored there.

**Graceful Skating ▷** *(Frictionless Motion)*  
When using Skate, you **don't have to move in a straight line**.

**Stormlight Reclamation ★** *(Reverse Abrasion)*  
At start of your turn, end any number of your infusions within reach and **recover their remaining Investiture**.

**Slippery Target ∞** *(Graceful Skating)*  
While infused with Abrasion: attacks can't graze you; Reactive Strikes against you gain disadvantage.

**Distant Surgebinding ∞** *(Reverse Abrasion)*  
Use your surges and their talents as though your **reach is 20 ft.** (no touch required).

**Slick Combatant ∞** *(Slippery Target)*  
While infused with Abrasion, you can interrupt a Move with other actions. Once per round, when you make a melee weapon attack mid-movement, roll an extra die of the same size as your Abrasion surge die as bonus damage. On a Complication, you fall Prone and end the Move.

**Smooth Operator ∞** *(Distant Surgebinding or Slippery Target)*  
While you have 1+ Investiture, you're always infused with Abrasion (no Investiture cost), and Skate costs 1 fewer focus.

## Adhesion *(Presence)*

**Orders:** Bondsmith, Windrunner  
*The surge that binds things together through Full Lashings.*

### Adhesion Surge *(▶)*
**Full Lashing:** Spend 1+ Investiture; touch two unsecured objects within reach and within 5 ft. of each other (must have a hand free). They become stuck together. Expends 1 Investiture per round.

One object can be a floor, wall, or ceiling (any size). Can't target characters, Invested objects, or Stormlight-infused objects.

**Adhesion on Others:** To Lash an unwilling target's items or surfaces supporting them, succeed on an Adhesion test vs. Physical defense.

**Breaking a Full Lashing:** Athletics test opposed by your Adhesion. Success = bond broken.

### Adhesion Talents

**Stormlight Reclamation ★** *(Speak the First Ideal)*  
End Adhesion infusions within reach and recover their remaining Investiture.

**Binding Strike ↻** *(Speak the First Ideal)*  
After hitting with a melee attack, spend ▶ or 2 focus to use Adhesion without spending an action. At least one target must be an object the target is wearing/holding. You auto-succeed on the test to infuse it.

**Binding Shot ★** *(Binding Strike)*  
Use Binding Strike when hitting with a **ranged attack** at any distance. No need to touch targets or have a hand free.

**Extended Adhesion ∞** *(Distant Surgebinding)*  
Full Lashings expend 1 Investiture per **Adhesion ranks** rounds instead of per round.

**Adhesive Trap ▶** *(Binding Shot)*  
Infuse a surface area (up to your surge size). Anyone touching it auto-becomes Fully Lashed to it.

**Living Adhesion ▶** *(Extended Adhesion)*  
You can use Adhesion on **characters directly**. When Lashed to something larger than them, they are Restrained; physical tests gain disadvantage; attacks against them gain advantage.

**Superior Bond ∞** *(Adhesive Trap or Extended Adhesion)*  
When a character tries to break your Full Lashing, if their Strength ≤ your Adhesion ranks, **you automatically succeed** the opposed test without rolling.

**Distant Surgebinding ∞** *(Stormlight Reclamation)*  
Use surges and talents as though reach is 20 ft. (Adhesion still requires Lashed objects within 5 ft. of each other).

## Cohesion *(Willpower)*

**Orders:** Stoneward, Willshaper  
*The surge that alters matter at the particle level. Commonly called Stoneshaping.*

### Cohesion Surge *(▶)*
Infuse 1+ Investiture into stone within reach (touch required, hand free). Stone becomes moldable like clay. Expends 1 Investiture per round. Can't exceed surge size. Can't target characters, Invested objects, or Stormlight-infused objects.

You can rough-shape the stone when activating; elaborate molding requires **Interact (▶)** or **Use a Skill (▶▶)** (with a DC set by GM for intricate work).

**Cohesion on Others:** Unwilling targets' held items or weight-supporting surfaces require a Cohesion test vs. Physical defense.

**Escaping Solid Stone:** When infusion ends, characters partially enclosed become Immobilized. To escape: Athletics test opposed by Cohesion. On failure: must be dug out (10+ minutes).

### Cohesion Talents

**Stone Spear ▶** *(Speak the First Ideal)*  
Spend 1 Investiture; ranged Cohesion attack vs. Physical (60 ft.), roll 2d4 impact (scales with ranks). On hit, spend Opportunity to knock target Prone.

**Memories of Stone ▶** *(Speak the First Ideal)*  
Spend 1 Investiture; communicate for 1 round with stone you're touching. Receive cryptic images/whispers about the area's history. GM may require Cohesion test to interpret.

**Sinkhole ▶▶** *(Stone Spear)*  
Spend 1 Investiture; choose an area of stone ground (up to your surge size) and targets standing on it. Test Cohesion once vs. each target's Cognitive. Success → they sink in, Immobilized.

**Tunneling ▶** *(Sinkhole)*  
Spend 1 Investiture to infuse yourself. Move through stone as difficult terrain, leaving a 5-ft-radius tunnel behind you.

**Through the Stone ∞** *(Sinkhole)*  
Use surges and talents as though reach is 20 ft. — as long as there's a stone surface from you to your target that you can touch.

**True Stoneshaping ★** *(Cohesion 2+, Tunneling)*  
When shaping stone, automatically reshape it to your will **without using additional actions or time** — raise/lower elevation, form walls, create difficult terrain, sculpt intricate shapes instantly.

**Flowing Earth ↻** *(True Stoneshaping)*  
After using Cohesion or spending ▶ on a Cohesion talent, shape stone beneath your feet to **push yourself** up to 5 ft. × Cohesion ranks in any direction (no Reactive Strikes triggered).

**Unbound Cohesion ∞** *(Cohesion 4+, Through the Stone)*  
Use Cohesion and its talents on **any solid material** that isn't alive, Invested, or Stormlight-infused.

## Division *(Intellect)*

**Orders:** Dustbringer, Skybreaker  
*The surge that decays and destroys.*

### Division Surge *(▶)*

**Targeting a Character:** Spend 1 Investiture; melee Division attack vs. Spiritual, 3d4 spirit damage (scales with ranks). If reduced to 0 health, target crumbles to dust and dies. On hit, add Division modifier to damage; spend 1 focus on a miss to graze.

**Targeting an Object or Area:** Spend Investiture equal to ranks needed for that size (1 for Small, 2 for Medium, etc.). In combat, must succeed on a Division test (see Under Pressure table). On success, target decays as you choose.

Can't target Invested or Stormlight-infused objects.

### Division Under Pressure (DC by Material)

| Material | DC |
|----------|----|
| Clear air | 0 |
| Vapors (smoke, gas) | 5 |
| Liquids (blood, water, oil) | 10 |
| Organics (flesh, bone, pulp) | 15 |
| Solids (metal, stone, crystal) | 20 |

To destroy an unwilling character's held object or the surface supporting them: use the higher of their Physical defense or the DC from the table above.

### Division Talents

**Bodily Decay ↻** *(Speak the First Ideal)*  
When you hit with a Division attack, spend Opportunity to cause one target to **suffer an injury**.

**Eroding Escape ▶** *(Speak the First Ideal)*  
Spend 1+ Investiture to end that many effects on yourself or an ally within reach that apply Immobilized, Restrained, or disadvantage on physical tests.

**Igniting Division ▶** *(Bodily Decay)*  
When affecting a target with Division, spend 1+ Investiture to set the target on fire for that many rounds: target becomes **Afflicted** (energy damage = Division modifier); area within 5 ft. becomes dangerous terrain (same damage). A character within reach can extinguish it (Use a Skill, Agility/Athletics vs. your Division).

**Spark Sending ∞** *(Bodily Decay or Eroding Escape)*  
Use Division and its talents as though reach is 20 ft., as long as there's a **solid surface** between you and the target.

**Gout of Flame ▶▶▶** *(Igniting Division)*  
Spend 3 Investiture; Division attack vs. Physical against each target in an area **one size larger** than normal, rolling 3d4 energy (scales with ranks).

**Inescapable Spark ∞** *(Spark Sending)*  
Spark Sending now extends to your **spren bond range**. No line of effect required (just sense your target). Targets can't benefit from Brace against your Division.

**Devastating Division ∞** *(Gout of Flame)*  
When rolling damage for Division, roll **one additional damage die**.

**Unleashed Entropy ∞** *(Spark Sending or Gout of Flame)*  
Division costs **one fewer ▶** (minimum 1). Division Under Pressure DCs reduced by 5.

## Gravitation *(Awareness)*

**Orders:** Skybreaker, Windrunner  
*The surge that changes the direction and magnitude of gravity. Creates Basic Lashings.*

### Gravitation Surge *(▶)*
**Basic Lashing:** Spend 1+ Investiture; touch a character or object within reach (hand free). Expends 1 Investiture per round. Your **gravitation rate** begins at 25 ft.

**Targeting a Willing Character:** They gain a flying rate equal to your gravitation rate for the duration.

**Infusing Yourself:** Gain same flying rate.

**Targeting an Object:** Declare a new gravity direction. Each round, move the object in a straight line up to your gravitation rate.

Can't target Invested objects or Stormlight-infused objects.

**Gravitation on Others (unwilling):** Gravitation test vs. Physical defense. On success, spend 1 Investiture to move them up to your gravitation rate in any direction. They become Restrained until start of your next turn. They can grab a large surface during movement (Agility test vs. your Gravitation).

If aloft when surge ends: fall and take normal falling damage.

### Gravitation Talents

**Flying Ace ▶** *(Speak the First Ideal)*  
While maintaining a Basic Lashing on yourself, fly up to your gravitation rate. Spend 1 focus during that flight to make a **melee weapon attack** mid-flight. Gravitation rate permanently increases to **40 ft**.

**Gravitational Slam ↻** *(Speak the First Ideal)*  
When using a Basic Lashing to move an unwilling target into a solid surface, they take **1d4 impact per 10 ft. moved** (scales with ranks). Target can Avoid Danger to halve damage.

**Stable Flight ∞** *(Flying Ace)*  
While maintaining a Basic Lashing on yourself, ranged attacks **don't gain disadvantage** from flying or unstable footing.

**Group Flight ★** *(Stable Flight)*  
Out of combat: when you infuse one character with a Basic Lashing, also infuse up to your Gravitation ranks in additional willing characters (within reach) at no extra Investiture.

**Lashing Shot ▶▶** *(Gravitational Slam)*  
Touch an unattended object (your surge size), then propel it at a target in line of effect. Spend Investiture = distance ÷ gravitation rate (rounded up). Ranged Gravitation attack vs. Physical; 2d4 impact (scales with ranks).

**Multiple Lashings ★** *(Gravitational Slam)*  
After successfully moving an unwilling character, infuse them with up to your Gravitation ranks in Investiture. The effect **continues for the duration** of the infusion (not just until start of your next turn).

**Aerial Squadron ▶** *(Gravitation 3+, Group Flight)*  
You can use **Group Flight in combat**.

**Master of the Skies ∞** *(Group Flight or Lashing Shot)*  
While you have 1+ Investiture, you're always infused with Gravitation (no Investiture expenditure required).

## Illumination *(Presence)*

**Orders:** Lightweaver, Truthwatcher  
*The surge that creates convincing illusions — visual and auditory. Known as Lightweaving.*

### Illumination Surge *(▶)*
Spend 1+ Investiture to Lightweave an illusion in thin air within your spren bond range.

- **Simple illusion (no movement/sound):** 1 Investiture per 10 minutes
- **Complex illusion (character or animated object):** 1 Investiture per round

**Disguising Yourself:** 0 Investiture; lasts until ended as ▷ or you run out of Investiture.

**Deceiving Others:**
- Simple objects/self-disguise: automatically convincing unless suspicious
- Complex illusions: GM may ask for Illumination test vs. target's Cognitive defense
- Scrutinizing character: Illumination test opposed by Perception test

**Controlling Illusions:** Can control all complex illusions within your spren bond range. Move up to your movement rate. Use ▷ for simple tasks; ▶ for attention-requiring tasks. Beyond bond range: illusions repeat their last task.

**Detecting Illusions:** Even if noticed, the illusion persists. White blurs appear where physical objects pass through illusions.

### Illumination Talents

**Distracting Illusion ▶** *(Speak the First Ideal)*  
Spend 1 Investiture; create an illusory duplicate of yourself or an ally within bond range. Attacks against that character gain **disadvantage and can't graze** — until one attack misses them or the scene ends.

**Lingering Lightweaving ★** *(Speak the First Ideal)*  
When Lightweaving, infuse the Investiture into a **sphere or unencased gem** (within 5 ft.). The illusion moves with the gem; it expends 1 Investiture per Illumination ranks rounds instead of per round.

**Disorienting Flash ▶** *(Distracting Illusion)*  
Spend 1 Investiture; project a burst of light in an area within reach. Illumination test vs. each target's Cognitive. On success: target becomes **Disoriented** until end of their next turn.

**Stormlight Reclamation ★** *(Distracting Illusion)*  
End Illumination infusions within spren bond range and **recover remaining Investiture**.

**Spiritual Illumination ▶▶** *(Disorienting Flash)*  
Spend 2 Investiture; create a momentary Lightweaving near an ally within bond range. They become **Determined and Focused** until end of their next turn.

**Multiplicative Lightweaving ★** *(Stormlight Reclamation)*  
When Lightweaving an illusion, create up to your Illumination ranks in **additional illusions** at no extra Investiture (same duration as the original).

**Painful Truth ▶▶** *(Spiritual Illumination)*  
Spend 2 Investiture; Illumination test vs. target's Spiritual. On success: they are Slowed until end of their next turn and must Move away from you at start of their next turn — or spend focus equal to your Illumination ranks to resist.

**Endless Illusions ∞** *(Multiplicative Lightweaving or Spiritual Illumination)*  
While 1+ Investiture, your Illumination infusions within spren bond range **expend no Investiture per turn**.

## Progression *(Awareness)*

**Orders:** Edgedancer, Truthwatcher  
*The surge that controls the growth and healing of living things. Growth for plants; Regrowth for characters.*

### Progression Surge *(▶)*
Touch a living thing within reach (hand free).

**Plant Growth:** Spend 1 Investiture; a plant rapidly grows to a chosen size (max: surge size OR species' natural maximum). One-time effect.

**Character Regrowth:** Infuse 1+ Investiture into a living character. They recover **1d4 + Progression modifier** health at the start of each of their turns (die scales with ranks). Expends 1 Investiture per round.

> Regrowth heals by aligning the Physical form with the Cognitive/Spiritual self — the target, not the healer, determines what is healed and how.

### Progression Talents

**Injury Regrowth ▶** *(Speak the First Ideal)*  
Spend 2 Investiture to instantly heal a willing target of a **temporary injury**; or 3 Investiture for a **permanent injury**.

**Explosive Growth ▶▶** *(Speak the First Ideal)*  
Spend 1 Investiture; use Growth in an area (up to surge size). Progression attack vs. Physical of targets in the area; roll 2d4 impact or keen (scales). Spend Opportunity to make any number of hit targets Immobilized until end of next turn.

**Swift Regeneration ∞** *(Progression 2+, Injury Regrowth)*  
When you use Regenerate, instead recover **1d6 + Progression modifier** health (die scales with ranks). While 1+ Investiture, you and your Regrowth targets can add your **Progression modifier to injury rolls**.

**Overgrowth ★** *(Explosive Growth)*  
When using Growth on a plant, test Progression DC 15 to make it grow **beyond its normal size limits** (up to surge size). On success: plant's health increases by 2d4 (scales).

**Extended Regrowth ∞** *(Swift Regeneration)*  
Regrowth infusions expend 1 Investiture per **Progression ranks** rounds instead of per round.

**Reliable Progression ★** *(Progression 2+, Overgrowth)*  
When you or a Regrowth target rolls a Progression die (Explosive Growth, Overgrowth, Swift Regeneration): if the result is lower than your Progression ranks, **set it equal to your Progression ranks**.

**From the Brink ▶▶▶** *(Progression 3+, Extended Regrowth)*  
Spend 3 Investiture; touch a character who died within the last minute. If willing, they **return to life** Unconscious with 0 health.

**Font of Life ∞** *(Extended Regrowth or Reliable Progression)*  
Using Progression and its talents costs **one fewer ▶** (minimum 1 action).

## Tension *(Strength)*

**Orders:** Bondsmith, Stoneward  
*The surge that alters the rigidity of objects.*

### Tension Surge *(▶)*
Spend 1+ Investiture; touch a soft object within reach (hand free). The object becomes **completely rigid** for the duration. Expends 1 Investiture per round.

Can't exceed surge size. Can't target characters, Invested objects, or Stormlight-infused objects.

You can rough-shape the material when activating the surge; elaborate work requires extra actions or a Tension skill test.

**Hardened Defense:** When infusing soft material worn by yourself or a willing character, their **Physical defense +2** (only one Tension surge at a time per character).

**Tension on Opponents:** Unwilling targets (wearing/wrapped in material) require Tension vs. Physical defense. On success: target becomes **Slowed** and gains disadvantage on all physical tests. They can break free via Athletics vs. your Tension.

### Tension Talents

**Tension Parry ↻** *(Speak the First Ideal)*  
Before you or an ally within reach is hit or grazed by an attack vs. Physical, spend Investiture to infuse their clothing. They gain the Hardened Defense effect (+2 Physical defense) — possibly turning a hit into a miss.

**Stormlight Reclamation ★** *(Speak the First Ideal)*  
End Tension infusions within reach and recover their remaining Investiture.

**Rigged Weaponry ▶** *(Tension Parry)*  
While wielding a melee weapon and holding 10+ ft. of flexible material, spend 1 Investiture to increase your weapon's reach by **10 ft.** until end of next turn. After hitting with a melee attack, spend ▶ or 2 focus to infuse Tension into an object the target is wearing/holding (auto-succeed).

**Extended Tension ∞** *(Stormlight Reclamation)*  
Tension infusions expend 1 Investiture per **Tension ranks** rounds instead of per round. While 1+ Investiture, you can freely maintain Tension infusions on objects you're holding/wearing.

**Cloth Mastery ★** *(Tension 2+, Rigged Weaponry)*  
When using Tension to create an object or weapon, instantly reshape it to any complexity **without extra actions or time** (intricate shapes, functioning armor, etc.).

**Surface Tension ▶** *(Extended Tension)*  
Use Tension on **liquids**, affecting up to twice your normal surge size. Surface tension increases so characters can **walk on it like solid ground**.

**Fine Control ▶** *(Cloth Mastery)*  
Infuse 1+ Investiture into an unattended soft object within reach (surge size). For the duration, the object has health (5 × Tension ranks) and defenses (10 + Tension modifier). While touching it, you can move it (up to 25 ft. along surfaces) and direct it with actions. Use ▷ for simple tasks; ▶ for complex ones. Melee attack using Tension vs. Physical: 2d4 impact (scales).

**Clothsmith ∞** *(Cloth Mastery or Surface Tension)*  
Hardened Defense grants **+4 Physical defense** instead of +2. Tension-created weapons gain an **extra d4 damage die** (scales with ranks).

## Transformation *(Willpower)*

**Orders:** Elsecaller, Lightweaver  
*The surge that transforms one material into another. Known as Soulcasting.*

### Transformation Surge *(▶)*
Choose a non-living object/surface within reach. Touch it (hand free). Make a **Transformation test** (see DC table below). On success, spend Investiture equal to size cost to transform it.

Can't exceed surge size. Can't target Invested objects or Stormlight-infused objects. Can't directly target living characters (without talents).

### Transformation Difficulty Classes (by material categories)

| | Solids | Organics | Liquids | Vapors | Clear Air | Flame* |
|---|--------|----------|---------|--------|-----------|--------|
| **Solids** | 10 | 10 | 15 | 20 | 25 | 30 |
| **Organics** | 10 | 10 | 10 | 15 | 20 | 25 |
| **Liquids** | 15 | 10 | 10 | 10 | 15 | 20 |
| **Vapors** | 20 | 15 | 10 | 10 | 10 | 15 |
| **Clear Air** | 25 | 20 | 15 | 10 | 10 | 10 |

*Flame only available with the Flamecasting talent.*

### Investiture Cost by Size
| Size | Cost |
|------|------|
| Small | 1 |
| Medium | 2 |
| Large | 3 |
| Huge | 4 |
| Gargantuan | 5 |

On failure: no Investiture spent; can't reattempt on that object this scene (unless you have Persistent Transformation).

**Peering into Shadesmar:** Spend 2 Investiture and Use a Skill; on success, use one Transportation effect to perceive 10 ft. around you in the Cognitive Realm.

### Transformation Talents

**Soulcast Defense ↻** *(Speak the First Ideal)*  
Before you or an ally within reach is hit by a projectile, spend 1 Investiture and test Transformation (DC = triggering attack). On failure: attack grazes. On success: attack misses; projectile becomes an Essence of your choice.

**Living Soulcasting ▶** *(Speak the First Ideal)*  
Spend 1 Investiture; melee Transformation attack vs. Spiritual of a living organism; 3d4 spirit damage (scales). If reduced to 0 health, they die and are transformed into a chosen material.

**Soulcast Parry ↻** *(Soulcast Defense)*  
Use Soulcast Defense on **melee weapon attacks** as well as ranged.

**Bloodcasting ▶** *(Living Soulcasting)*  
Spend 1 Investiture; DC 15 Transformation test while touching a character. On success: **cleanse all poison** from target; reduce one injury's recovery time by 5 days.  
On Complication: target's max health −(2 × level) until after their next long rest.

**Distant Surgebinding ∞** *(Soulcast Parry)*  
Use surges and talents as though reach is 20 ft. (no touch required).

**Flamecasting ∞** *(Soulcast Parry)*  
Add "Flame" as a sixth category (DC 30 from Stone). When Soulcasting to flame, also make an attack vs. Physical against each character within 5 ft. of the object; 2d4 energy (scales).

**Persistent Transformation ★** *(Transformation 2+, Distant Surgebinding)*  
When transforming non-living material, max DC is **15** (not unlimited). Can reattempt Soulcasting after failure in the same scene, but each success on a previously-failed object costs 1 extra Investiture per prior failure.

**Expansive Transmuter ∞** *(Flamecasting)*  
Soulcasting non-living material costs **2 fewer Investiture** (minimum 1).

## Transportation *(Intellect)*

**Orders:** Elsecaller, Willshaper  
*The surge that enables looking into and transporting characters between realms.*

### Transportation Surge *(▶)*
Spend 1+ Investiture to peer into the **Cognitive Realm** at your current location. For each Investiture spent, use one of these effects within your spren bond range:

| Effect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Learn Emotions/Motives** | Observe spren around people to discern true feelings or motives; may grant advantage or auto-reveal motivation |
| **Locate Characters** | Watch for people's flames in Shadesmar to guess their location in the Physical Realm |
| **Sense Investiture** | Reveal Invested objects, Fused, other Radiants, etc. — their "true nature" visible in the Cognitive Realm |

Some things require a Transportation test at GM discretion.

### Transportation Talents

**Cognitive Farsight ∞** *(Speak the First Ideal)*  
Perceive in the Cognitive Realm up to **3 × spren bond range**. While 1+ Investiture: always know **which direction is north** and the direction to the nearest settlement.

**Cognitive Vision ★** *(Cognitive Farsight)*  
Additional options when peering into the Cognitive Realm:
- **Learn Intent:** Transportation test vs. enemy's Cognitive; on success, learn their likely intentions and share with allies. Target gains disadvantage on their next test against you or an ally.
- **Locate Objects:** Touch soul beads to learn about nearby objects or environmental details.

**Realmic Evasion ↻** *(Speak the Second Ideal)*  
Before being hit by an attack, spend 1 Investiture and test Transportation (DC = attack's test result). On failure: hit becomes a graze. On success: attack misses.

**Realmic Step ▶▶** *(Realmic Evasion)*  
Test Transportation DC 15; on success, spend 2 Investiture to teleport (along with everything you're wearing/carrying) to an unoccupied space within your spren bond range. No Reactive Strikes.  
If over a large body of water: costs only 1 Investiture.

**Elsecalling ▶** *(Speak the Third Ideal, Realmic Step)*  
Transport yourself between the Physical and Cognitive Realms (along with worn/carried items). No Reactive Strikes.
- Physical → Cognitive: spend 1 Investiture (no test needed)
- Cognitive → Physical: DC 20 test; spend 2 Investiture on success

**Shared Transportation ★** *(Transportation 4+, Elsecalling)*  
When you transport yourself via Elsecalling or Realmic Step, spend 1 Investiture per additional willing character you bring along.

**Elsegate ▶▶** *(Speak the Fourth Ideal, Shared Transportation)*  
After a long rest, instantly transport yourself and up to 10 companions to a previously-visited **Oathgate or permanent perpendicularity**. Must spend infused spheres worth 2 marks per character transported (all become dun on arrival).

**Realmwalker ∞** *(Elsecalling)*  
When you test Transportation for a talent, **you automatically succeed**.
